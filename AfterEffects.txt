01 //////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////01
//Create new composition from mp4
    1. Drag and drop mp4 from Project panel and into the 'Create new composition' icon (next to folder icon) located within the Project panel itself
    2. You can also head to Menu>Composition>New composition
    3. Adjust the duration of the composition in duration section
    4. Framerate is usually 29.97
    5. Preset is HDTV 1080 29.97
    6. Background color refers to the base layer
    7. Scroll mouse button to zoom in or zoom out
    IMPORTANT NOTE: Drag and drop mp4 to the new composition icon in order to match up all the properties and framerates of the video.
    IMPORTANT NOTE:  Make sure that the start timecode is at 0,00,00,00. to align to the clip. Or it will follow the camera time code by default and tracking will be difficult

    //1. If the video was recorded in higher frame rate e.g120fps, the video in AE will be in slowmotion
    //2. Speed the video up by lowering the computer lag.
    //3. To do this, within the composition panel, between colour management icon and the transparency grid toggle icon, there's a drop down menu
    //4. Menu>Auto, Full, Half, Third, Quarter
    //5. Full is high quality playback and quarter is low quality playback (less laggy)

//Video playing inside letters effects
    //Add a video to composition
    //Add a really thick bolded texts into the composition
    //Inside composition list panel, under video's ".trkMat" column, select "AlphaMatte" from the dropdown menu
    //You can also have every letter of the word to play different videos by shrinking the video
    //Use dropShadow on the text, set dropShadow opacity to 100%,  Distance to 24.0, softness to 63.0
    
    //Precompose composition with hard dark shadow
        //Delete dropShadow effects
        //Highlight both text and video element, right-click and select pre-compose
        //Add dropShadow effect to precomposed element
        //set pdropShadow opacity to 100%, distance to 14 and softness to 60
        //Under Project panel, select dropShadow and duplicate (ctrl+D)
        //To change the text, double click the pre-comp layer and the video and text will popup again in the composition panel

14 //////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////14
//3D text animation
    //Create new composition with Cinematic 4D turned on
    //Write a bold text
    //In timeline panel, turn on 3D Layer
    //Drop down layer, find geometry and increase Extrusion depth to 75.00
    //From menu, select Layer>New>Light>Create new light effect 
    //Hold shift and drag light to zoom light out from object - You can test this by rotating
    //Position the text to start point of of the animation
    //In timeline panel, under text layer, dropdown layer and open the "Transform" layer
    //Switch on "Orientation" by clicking on the stopwatch, this will create a keyframe
    //Insert keyframes as you desire and play
    

15 //////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////14
//Write on text effect
    //Write a text on a new composition
    //On the right hand panel, navigate to the effects tab
    //Open Generate>Write-on
    //Or alternatively, search for "Write-On" and apply to the text
    //On the left hand side project panel, change color to red
    //A small circular brush will appear at thecenter of the text
    //Resize the brush "BrushSize" on the project panel to 40
    //Position the brush to initial position
    //activate keyframe effects>write-on>BrushPosition
    //Insert all the keyframes
    //Change "PaintStyle" from "OriginalImage" to "RevealOriginalImage"
    //Play


16 //////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////12  
//Combining WriteOn Effects and In-Video playing inside letters effects
    //New composition, add in a short video
        //First layer,will be the video
        //Copy over the video to make second layer, drag video to the end of first layer and lock it with "N"
        //Rightclick on the second layer and under the menu, select time>time-reverseLayer
        //Now that you have a video that plays and reverses itself
        //add in third layer video with the first layer video so it plays again at the end after it reverses
        //Highlight all three of the layers with a box and right-click
        //select "pre-compose" to form a single video layer.
    //Write a text over the composition
    //Under timeline panel, change precompose " .TrkMat" to AlphaMatte
    //Select both layers and precompose them both one more time 
    //Go to the right-hand panel, find write-on effects under effects tab
        //Drop it to the composition
        //Change write-On color to red in the Project panel
        //Resize the brush "BrushSize" on the project panel to 40
        //Position the brush to initial position
        //activate keyframe effects>write-on>BrushPosition
        //Insert all the keyframes
        //Change "PaintStyle" from "OriginalImage" to "RevealOriginalImage"
        //Play
    //You can also add in drop shadow Perspective>DropShadow
        //Opacity=100, Distance=19, Softness=58
        //Press CTRL+D to  duplicate the dropshadow effect
        //Set Distance=1.0, softness=30.0 for the second dropShadow
        //Play
    //You can also add in "Scale" effect so the text will be expanding as it animates
    //Add in color correction by opening the precomposes to the video
        //Add in ColorCorrection>Vibrance to the clip
        //Turn up vibrancet o 100 on the project panel
    //Add in brightness effect to the composition ColorCorrection>Brightness&Contrast to the first clips
        //Play
